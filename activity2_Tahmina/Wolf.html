The Wolf

Bloodthirsty and loyal!

There are seven species of wolves. They include the Gray, Arctic, Eastern, Ethiopian, Indian, Red and Himalayan Wolf. They are the largest member of the dog family. They tend to live in a pack, who serve as their family and protectors. Wolves have extremely powerful jaws. Fully grown wolves have a total of 42 teeth in their mouth. They have the ability to create a crushing pressure of about 1,500 pounds per square inch. That’s twice the pressure that a dog’s jaw creates. Wolves are carnivorous and commonly feed on deer, moose and bison. They are great hunters and can be as fast as 38 miles per hour. A wolf’s average life span ranges from 6 to 13 years.

Fun Facts!

  • A single wolf can eat up to 40 pounds of meat in one sitting.
  • Once two wolves mate, they are together until one of the wolves dies.
  • The alphas are usually the caretakers of the pack, they play the role of mother and father.
  • Wolves have approximately 200 million scent cells. This gives them the ability to smell other animals from a mile away.
  • When the United States Endangered Species Act was signed into law in 1973, wolves were the first animal to be placed on the list for protection.
